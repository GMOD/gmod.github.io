# <span id="DroSpeGe:_rapid_access_database_for_new_Drosophila_species_genomes" class="mw-headline">DroSpeGe: rapid access database for new Drosophila species genomes</span>

Paper by [Don Gilbert](User:Dongilbert "User:Dongilbert") in Nucleic
Acids Research, 2007, Vol. 35, Database issue.

## <span id="Abstract" class="mw-headline">Abstract</span>

The Drosophila species [comparative
genome](Category:Comparative_Genomics "Category:Comparative Genomics")
database DroSpeGe
(<a href="http://insects.eugenes.org/" class="external free"
rel="nofollow">http://insects.eugenes.org/</a> DroSpeGe/) provides
genome researchers with rapid, usable access to 12 new and old
Drosophila genomes, since its inception in 2004. Scientists can use,
with minimal computing expertise, the wealth of new genome information
for developing new insights into insect evolution. New genome assemblies
provided by several sequencing centers have been annotated with known
model organism gene homologies and gene predictions to provided basic
comparative data. [TeraGrid](Genome_grid "Genome grid") supplies the
shared cyberinfrastructure for the primary computations. This genome
database includes homologies to Drosophila melanogaster and eight other
eukaryote model genomes, and gene predictions from several groups.
[BLAST](Category:BLAST "Category:BLAST") searches of the newest
assemblies are integrated with genome maps.
[GBrowse](GBrowse.1 "GBrowse") maps provide detailed views of
cross-species aligned genomes. [BioMart](BioMart "BioMart") provides for
data mining of annotations and sequences. Common chromosome maps
identify major synteny among species. Potential gain and loss of genes
is suggested by Gene Ontology groupings for genes of the new species.
Summaries of essential genome statistics include sizes, genes found and
predicted, homology among genomes, phylogenetic trees of species and
comparisons of several gene predictions for sensitivity and specificity
in finding new and known genes.
